True 3D geometry representation decision

Selected representation:
- Piao-style RBC six parameters as the primary first-pilot representation.
- Parameters: L, W, D, wLD, wWD, wLW.
- The pilot is single-defect and single-component only.
- Derived arrays are saved for training and QA: depth grid, projected depth map, projected footprint mask.

Why RBC first:
- It is low-dimensional and auditable.
- It aligns with the Piao-style route without claiming full Piao reproduction.
- It avoids immediately returning to dense/free-form grid targets.
- It provides explicit length, width, depth, and shape-curvature parameters that can be compared with geometry metrics.
- It can be used later by Piao-style feature regression or Priewald-style constrained refinement.

Rejected for first pilot:
- Bézier/spline surface as primary representation: too flexible for the first identifiability smoke.
- depth-map/profile DOF grid as primary representation: high-dimensional and likely to repeat dense decoder problems.
- RBF/basis profile as primary representation: possible second-stage compromise, but not first.
- multi-component 3D set: deferred until single-component true 3D is proven.
- polygon/free-form 3D: deferred because it weakens alignment with Piao RBC and increases COMSOL QA risk.

Important boundary:
- Selecting RBC as the design representation does not mean the current COMSOL generator can already generate RBC variable-depth solids.
- 20.66 must prove the geometry construction path before any training or pack generation.
