## Current judgment

- `cap128_area3_bce7` has better average IoU and lower average predicted defect area than `cap32_area3_bce7`.
- `cap128_area3_bce7` has worse average `mu_mse` and `mu_mae` in this 100-sample run.
- Sample-level stability is not sufficient for promotion: `cap128` improves 45/100 samples and regresses on 55/100 samples.
- The most defensible current default capacity remains `32x2 + area3_bce7`; `128x4 + area3_bce7` should remain a high-capacity diagnostic candidate rather than the 200x100 default.
- Boundary unchanged: BCE is a semi-supervised / diagnostic upper bound, not unsupervised weak-form success.

## Next-step recommendation

- Do not promote `128x4` as the default capacity from S47 alone.
- If continuing capacity work, inspect the `cap128` regression samples 75, 44, 21, 40, 94, 71, 43, 98, 28, and 60 before running more broad sweeps.
- For default reporting, keep `area3_bce7` with `32x2` as the stable 200x100 semi-supervised runner unless a later targeted change improves both average metrics and sample-level stability.
