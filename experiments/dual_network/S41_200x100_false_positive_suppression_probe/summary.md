## Current Judgment

- Best false-positive suppression by lowest average `defect_area_pred`: `area3_bce7` (1.074100e+03).
- Best average IoU: `area3_bce7` (8.209086e-01).
- Best average mu_mae: `area3_bce7` (1.641459e+02).
- Best overall IoU / area / mu_mae tradeoff: `area3_bce7`.

Compared with S39 `temp25_lambda5_outer60`, `area3_bce7` improves average IoU, lowers average predicted defect area, and lowers both mu_mse and mu_mae. This indicates that strengthening BCE together with a moderate area prior can suppress false positives without harming recall on this S39 200x100 / 100-sample set.

The pure area-prior increase is less convincing: `area3_bce5` lowers predicted area and slightly improves IoU versus S39, while `area5_bce5` improves mu errors but does not reduce area as much as `area3_bce5` or `area3_bce7`. Continuing to blindly increase area-prior weight is therefore not recommended.

Boundary condition: these results remain semi-supervised BCE mask-prior diagnostic upper bounds, not unsupervised weak-form success.

Use `area3_bce7` as the next 200x100 candidate if this branch continues. The next useful work is failure-sample taxonomy, structural false-positive suppression, or post-processing analysis rather than blind radius / centers / area-prior scans.
